Hannah Haberkern, PhD

Principal Investigator

I'm a neuroscientist interested in understanding how animals navigate in complex and dynamic environments. In my freetime I sew quilts and garments and go running. Learn more about me on my personal website.

Originally from Germany, I studied biomedicine at the University of Würzburg, before transitioning to a masters in Computational Biology and Bioinformatics at the ETH/University Zurich. For my PhD I worked with Berthold Hedwig at the University of Cambridge and Vivek Jayaraman at HHMI Janelia. I stayed at Janelia for my postdoc, before returning to Würzburg to start this lab.

Romita Trehan, MSc.

PhD Student (shared with Ache Lab, Würzburg)

I am an aspiring neuroscientist with a keen interest in studying flexible decision-making processes and how these help animals to adapt to their environment. I have experience working with Drosophila, conducting behaviour assays to study the impact of environmental stimuli on sleep and navigation. Apart from my research, I enjoy exploring new avenues for personal growth. I love reading, nurturing my green thumb, and exploring new places.

I hold a Master's degree in Applied Biosciences and Biotechnology from Imperial College London, and I have experience working in science communication and outreach. Originally from India, I moved to London for my master's and am now pursuing a joint PhD in the Haberkern and Ache Labs at the University of Würzburg.

Are you curious about the neural mechanisms that allow insects to navigate a complex and highly dynamic world despite having just a tiny brain? We currently have open positions!

Undergraduate students

Interested in doing a practical, bachelor or master thesis in our lab? Please send Hannah an email with your research interests.

PhD students

We have fully-funded 4-year PhD positions. To learn more about the projects and to apply, please contact Hannah. Please include a brief introduction of yourself covering training, past research experiences and current research interests.

Postdocs

We have one fully-funded postdoc position for 4 years. The ideal candidate would have prior experience in neurophysiology but we are open to any candidates with a PhD in the life sciences or engineering. If you are interested, please email Hannah and include a brief description for why you are interested in the lab, and what your goals and expectations for a postdoc are. Please also include a CV and a manuscript that illustrates your past research.
